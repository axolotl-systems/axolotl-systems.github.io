Make ML deployment boring

Most AI/ML projects fail because teams treat them like research instead of software. Models work in notebooks but never make it to production.

I've managed teams that deployed ML models multiple times a day at Spotify and DPG Media. Now I help other companies build the same capabilities so they can constantly optimize the metrics that actually matter.

PhD in Search, previously Engineering Manager at Spotify where my teams deployed 50+ recommendation model updates per month to 400M+ users. At DPG Media, I built the infrastructure that let data scientists deploy models in minutes instead of weeks.

Where these opinions come from

At Spotify, I managed teams that deployed to millions of users daily. The platform made everything self-service—getting a new cluster, scaling up, or running A/B tests took minutes, not weeks. Speed was the default, not the exception.

Before that, at Google, DPG Media, and Blendle, I saw how different approaches to engineering scale. Some companies move fast because they invest in the right infrastructure. Others get stuck because they treat every deployment like a research project.

My PhD taught me that search algorithms are fundamentally about user behavior and business metrics—not just relevance scores. The same applies to ML: if you can't deploy and measure quickly, your sophisticated models don't matter.

I'm opinionated because I've seen what works at scale. Platform teams, golden paths, and self-service infrastructure aren't nice-to-haves—they're competitive advantages.
